{% trans 'PV on commercial buildings' context 'Market UK: section header.' %}

{% blocktrans context 'Solar market UK.' %} The BRE (Building Research Establishment) National Solar Center released a report on solar PV installations on commercial buildings in the UK: {% endblocktrans %} BRE (2016) Solar PV on commercial buildings: a guide for owners and developers, (K. Arora, S. Diu, J. Roper and G. Hartnell).

{% blocktrans context 'Solar market UK.' %}The following is a summary of their findings:{% endblocktrans %}

  • {% trans 'The UK has an estimated 250,000 hectares of south facing commercial roof space, which could provide roughly 50% of the UK\'s electricity demand if utilized with photovoltaics (PV).' context '' %}
  • {% trans 'In contrast to most other European countries commercial installations in the UK account for only a little more than one quarter of the total generated solar energy of 4.1GW.' context '' %}
  • {% trans 'Power Purchase Agreements (PPA) typically involve a lot of legal complications that account for the relatively low rate of commercial rooftop installations to date.' %}
  • {% trans 'In the UK around 6000-8000m2 of sloping roof space is needed to accomodate 1MW PV capacity. Flat rooftops require a little more area to avoid shading between each of the panels.' %}
  • {% trans 'The consumption profile of the building(s) on site is key to the financial viability of the project. Generally, the higher the self-consumption percentage, the better the profit.' %}
